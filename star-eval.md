## Findings (2026-07-06)

1. **There is no real "easy" band.** A star-board puzzle solvable by tier 1
   alone needs ~96% of cells given (vs 35% classic): the star kills the local
   `[X,X,_] → ¬X` deduction (it only *eliminates* a colour now), so tier 1
   fires roughly once per puzzle. The variant's difficulty floor is classic
   "medium". The web UI still offers Easy, but it deals a nearly-full board —
   consider hiding Easy for star boards or relabelling the bands.
2. **The texture flips from windows to counting.** Classic solves are driven
   by tier 1 (~14 invocations vs ~7 tier 2 on 6-6 medium/hard); star solves are
   almost pure tier 2 (~10-40 invocations, tier 1 ≈ 0) — line counts plus
   star placement (hidden singles). The hypothesised two-phase feel is real:
   locate the star, then the line collapses to binary Unruly.
3. **How much harder?** At the same band label, star puzzles carry more clues
   (medium 27-29% of cells vs 22%; hard 25-26% vs 20%) yet lean on heavier
   tactics throughout, and the raw puzzle space is far less logic-friendly:
   uniqueness-minimal puzzles need guessing 3/6 times on s7 and 5/6 on s9 (0/6
   classic). Rough net effect: star-medium sits between classic medium and
   hard; star-hard is a notch above classic hard at equal cell counts.
4. **Generation cost is fine up to s11; s15 hard is slow.** ~7 s on s11 hard
   but ~24 s on s15 hard (a single attempt overshoots the 7 s web budget,
   which is only checked between attempts). If s15 hard feels sluggish in the
   web app, give star boards their own `generationBudget` curve.
5. **Verdict on "fun" (to confirm by playtest):** medium/hard star boards are
   well-posed, uniquely solvable, and hint-walkable end-to-end; the star adds
   a genuinely new deduction (both pencils blocked ⇒ star; last star slot ⇒
   star) rather than just more grind. The risk is the missing gentle band:
   there are almost no "gimme" opening moves, so s7-medium is the recommended
   entry point.
